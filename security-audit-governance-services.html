FAQ

Common questions about security audits

Can't find your answer? Contact us and we'll get back to you within 24 hours.

A security audit is a systematic review of your security controls, policies, and configurations against a standard or framework. A penetration test actively attempts to exploit vulnerabilities to demonstrate real-world impact. Both are complementary — audits identify gaps in your controls, while pen tests prove whether those gaps are exploitable.

We recommend a comprehensive security audit at least annually, with penetration testing every 6-12 months. Additionally, audits should be triggered by major changes — new product launches, cloud migrations, acquisitions, or significant architecture changes. Regulated industries (fintech, healthcare) often require more frequent assessments.

We design all assessments to minimize disruption. Passive audits and configuration reviews have zero impact on production. Active penetration tests are conducted in agreed windows, with clear rules of engagement and rollback procedures. We always coordinate timing with your team to avoid business-critical periods.

A security governance framework includes security policies and procedures, risk management processes, asset classification, access control standards, incident response plans, business continuity planning, security awareness training programs, and metrics for measuring security effectiveness. We tailor the framework to your industry, size, and regulatory requirements.
